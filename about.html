Growing Up

I was born in November of 2002 to Mexican immigrant parents in the Chicagoland area of Illinois. My parents worked hard every day to put a roof over me and my two sisters ' heads. My parents always preached to me about the value of an education and pushed me to do well in school. I will forever be grateful to them for instilling in me the values of hard work as well as Catholicism. In elementary school, the teachers noticed that I excelled at math and science, and oftentimes I would sit outside and work on the assignments by myself because I was so far ahead compared to my classmates. This isolation made me lonely so I picked up baseball and basketball and I made a lot of my friends through sports which I still play to this day.

Learning To Love Technology

I always had a thing for tech. I learned to play video games at the same time I learned to speak and walk. I grew up playing my sister's Nintendo 64, eventually getting a Gameboy and Nintendo D.S. By the time I was nine I was using my sister's Samsung tablet to illegally download Minecraft for free by jailbreaking the device. I always had a creative mind. So in 5th grade, I applied for the Magnet Academy in my city which was a 2nd-8th grade school for math and science, and was accepted. They gave every student a personal laptop to take home and complete homework with. When I was in 5th grade I was introduced to programming via Code.Org and fell in love. I used to work on creating video games and loved the challenge of getting it all to work. In 7th and 8th I took robotics classes where we would program these Lego robots to complete obstacle courses and again, I fell in love with it. Working on a problem untill I got the robot to work right was just so satisfying to me. This love for problem-solving using technology and robotics is what led me to where I am today.

College and Beyond

During my senior year of college, I decided I would pursue my higher education at Dominican University in River Forest, IL. To me, it just felt like the right thing to do. It was a religious private school located in a city secluded by nature and only 15 minutes away from Downtown Chicago. By this point, I was already interested in investing so I could not decide between majoring in either Comp Sci or Business Administration. After sitting down with a counselor at Dominican and explaining my situation, he recommended that I major in Informatics. Informatics is essentially the crossroads of Comp Sci, Business, Information Systems, and Data Science. I decided to go with it and haven't looked back since. I truly am grateful for the support of my Informatics advisors Don Hamerly and Hassan Zamir. My advisors have supported me greatly along the way. In the summer of 2023, I helped my advisor Hassan Zamir, who is the director of the Informatics program at Dominican University with a research project. As a research assistant, I used reference management software to analyze peer-reviewed papers and come up with conclusions on the use cases, benefits, and downfalls of Artificial intelligence systems in the education sector. I have also been an active member of the Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics club. I am proud to say that my accomplishments led me to my next venture, a 2024 summer internship at CDW in the downtown Chicago location. CDW is one of the country's top IT solutions companies. I am excited to see what the future holds.
